Can we segment hotel bookings into meaningful customer clusters to predict cancellation risk, 
booking behavior, and upsell opportunities?
--------------
Why it’s relevant:
Hotel managers need to understand different types of customers:

Who is likely to cancel? -> Helps reduce last-minute losses.

Who books for longer stays or brings more guests? -> Helps optimize pricing and promotions.

Who prefers certain meal plans, room types, or booking channels? -> Helps personalize offers.

Goal: Identify clusters of bookings/customers with similar patterns, using unsupervised learning, to inform targeted marketing, risk management, and operational planning.
--------------

Useful columns
-------------
-------------
Customer behavior / booking features (numerical + categorical):
-------------
lead_time -> Time between booking and arrival
stays_in_weekend_nights, stays_in_week_nights -> Stay duration patterns

adults, children, babies -> Group size

previous_cancellations, previous_bookings_not_canceled -> Historical behavior

booking_changes -> Flexibility / risk behavior

days_in_waiting_list -> Patience / booking strategy

total_of_special_requests -> Preferences / spending behavior

Categorical features:
---------------
hotel -> City vs. resort property

meal -> Meal plan preference

market_segment -> Source of booking

distribution_channel -> How they booked

deposit_type -> Risk management

customer_type -> Contract, transient, group

reserved_room_type, assigned_room_type -> Room preferences

Optional derived features:
----------------
total_guests = adults + children + babies

stay_length = stays_in_week_nights + stays_in_weekend_nights

-----------------
Analysis / Clustering Goals
-----------------
Customer Segmentation: Group customers by booking patterns, stay preferences, and risk behavior.

Cancellation Risk Profiling: Clusters should reveal high-risk vs. low-risk bookings.

Revenue Optimization & Upselling: Identify clusters likely to book longer stays or request premium services.

Marketing Strategy: Tailor offers or communication for different clusters.
